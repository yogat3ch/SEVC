The giving of Dhamma Service, whether on a course or in some other way to help the spread of Dhamma, is one of the most valuable experiences a student of Vipassana can have. Not only is it rewarding to know that you have helped others to taste the Nectar of Dhamma, it also is a wonderful source of growth and strength in one's own development in meditation practice. Without the voluntary assistance of Old Students who feel a volition to give to others a little of what they have received from their participation in Vipassana courses, the courses cannot be arranged and conducted. Goenkaji has spoken often on the purpose of Dhamma service and teaches that it is an integral part of an Old Student's growth and development on this path. Giving Dhamma service in accordance with the Code of Conduct for Dhamma Workers strengthens a student of Vipassana for living a Dhamma life in the outside world. There are opportunities for all Old Students to participate and gain the wonderful benefit of giving Dhamma Service in several different ways.

Serving on Courses

If you can volunteer your time serving one or more 10-day courses either full-time or part-time, please complete the Dhamma Service Application On-line when applying for a course. (Use the standard on-line application and mark the box for Dhamma Service).

Serving on a Regular Basis at the Center

Old Students are needed who can serve regularly during the especially busy days of a course such as at the beginning to help get the kitchen up and running smoothly and at the end of a course to help coordinate the clean up with the students.  Please contact the Center if you are able to come to the Center on a regular basis (every 2 weeks, once a month, or every other month) to help coordinate during the busy days of a course's beginning and end.  Additionally, you may serve during the “Between Course Service Period” to get the Center cleaned and reset for the next course.  If you have two or three days to serve monthly or bi-monthly, we'd love to hear from you, and familiarize you with the Center so you may help coordinate activities during these important periods.

Serving During Work Periods (Spring, Summer, Fall and Winter)

Dhamma Service Work Periods have a more relaxed schedule that includes three daily group sittings, work on seasonal projects, and special evening discourses. Projects include gardening, light maintenance, sewing, deep cleaning and more complex skilled projects that can't be done during courses. There is often an opportunity to meet with a local assistant teacher, as well as the opportunity to serve with many fellow meditators over three delicious meals a day.  This program is for all Old Students who have sat at least one course with S.N. Goenka or one of his assistant teachers and who have not practiced another meditation technique since their last course. Such Old Students may serve all or part of the program. View the schedule and apply here.

Long-Term Servers

Long-term servers have been the sustaining force at the Center. The long-term Dhamma server program is set up to accommodate those who have from two to twelve months to spend giving service at the Center. Servers begin by applying for a couple of courses, and if the Center atmosphere is mutually beneficial they may then complete a long-term server application and have an interview with the Center Management and the Center Teachers. Most long-term servers serve on courses, however, after some time they may move into other work such as landscaping, office or committee work. Presently, the Center has long-term server cabins for those staying at the Center for extended periods of time. ---- View an informative Zoom webinar on opportunities for Long-Term Servers! A major role for Dhamma servers is in Center management. Periodically, openings become available for Center managers and other management roles. Interested persons may contact the Center directly to begin discussions.
