“My concentration level went up and I could also have control over my anger to a certain extent” -14 yr. old

Children and teenagers ages 8-15 learn the technique of Anapana meditation as taught by S.N. Goenka. Anapana is the observation of the natural breath as it comes in and goes out. Young people who have started practicing this technique often experience better concentration, sharper memory, improved comprehension, and the ability to stay calmer. In short, they gain a practical tool to use in the face of adversity or challenge. The course includes meditation instruction, art and outdoor activities.

Feature Article:  Dawn of a New Millennium
In previous issues, we chronicled Goenkaji’s teaching journey decade by decade. From this issue, we will focus on one year at a time, starting with the pivotal year 2000.

First Black Heritage Course in the USA
On January 3, 2010, the first worldwide African Heritage Course concluded in India at Dhamma Paṭṭana, the center next to the Global Pagoda. Goenkaji strongly welcomed this initiative because, in his words, “White, black or brown, a human being is a human being.” His wish was for people of every background to experience liberation from suffering. In August 2010, Dhamma Dīpa in the UK held another African Heritage Course.

Annual Old Student Meeting
Last month, nearly sixty old students gathered at Dhamma Patapa for the Annual Old Student Meeting.  After two years of virtual meetings, it was a wonderful opportunity to see everyone in person and share the familiar activities of past years such as the Friday evening ice-breaker and open space discussion groups with the topics being chosen by those in attendance. Saturday morning featured a talk by Bruce Stewart entitled “Nurturing and Supporting Our Practice”. The talk was followed by small groups discussing and reflecting upon some of the points of the talk. For a transcript of the talk, please read the article below that includes a link. An old student shared her experience this way:
Attending the annual old student meeting this year was such a transformative experience for me. Not only did I get to reconnect with old friends and make some new ones, but it also helped reinvigorate my daily practice. One of the highlights of the meeting was a talk that Bruce gave on the topic of nurturing/supporting our practice. He suggested that inquiring into our deepest reason for practice – and returning to this reason when sitting is challenging – is one way to help ourselves. Having a ‘mission statement’ for my practice has been so beneficial for me, and I remind myself of it every day before I sit.”
We hope you’ll be able to join next year from November 3-5, 2023.
